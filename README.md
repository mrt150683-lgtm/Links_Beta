# Links Beta

> Capture it. Connect it. Actually use it.

Links Beta is a local-first AI research workspace for people dealing with messy information, half-finished ideas, evidence trails, scattered notes, and too many tabs.

It helps you collect material, organise it into isolated project spaces, and turn raw inputs into something usable: searchable context, linked evidence, summaries, questions, patterns, and deeper research workflows.

This is not pretending to be magic.  
It is a serious beta with a lot already working, a lot being refined, and a lot more still coming.

---

## What Links Beta Is

Links is a private, modular research and intelligence system built around a simple idea:

**your data should stay under your control.**

You can capture text, links, documents, images, audio, notes, and research threads into separate project spaces called **pots**.

From there, Links can help you explore:

- what something is about
- who or what is mentioned
- what connects to what
- what looks important
- what is missing
- what deserves a deeper look

It is designed for people who want more than a chatbot and less than a bloated corporate surveillance platform.

## What It Does

### 1. Capture Into Isolated Project Spaces
Store research inside separate **pots** so projects do not bleed into each other.

Capture can include:

- text snippets
- saved links
- PDFs and documents
- images and screenshots
- audio and transcripts
- notes and chat context

### 2. Process Material in the Background
Once content is in, Links can process it in the background and turn it into structured, usable layers such as:

- tags
- summaries
- extracted entities
- linked relationships
- searchable context
- research artifacts

### 3. Keep Originals Separate From AI Output
Original captures stay original. AI-generated outputs are treated as derived layers, not rewritten truth.

That matters when you are working with evidence, nuance, or anything that should not be silently “improved” into nonsense.

### 4. Let You Explore With AI Chat
Each project space can become a working context for chat, questioning, summarising, comparing, and analysis.

Instead of throwing random prompts into the void and hoping for the best, the system is built to work with the material you actually saved.

### 5. Run Deeper Research Workflows
Links is also aimed at more advanced research flows, including:

- structured question generation
- deeper report building
- cross-source analysis
- contradiction spotting
- follow-up paths and open loops
- project planning and intelligence generation

### 6. Stay Local-First and Privacy-Conscious
The philosophy is simple:

**your research should not become somebody else’s training data, product telemetry, or quiet little goldmine.**

## Why It Is Different

A lot of AI tools want to be your everything while quietly owning your data, your workflow, and your future migration pain.

Links goes in a different direction:

- local-first by design
- privacy-respecting
- modular instead of locked down
- built around provenance and auditability
- focused on real research and real workflows
- flexible model choice rather than one-provider dependency
- designed to grow into a serious personal or professional intelligence environment

In plain English: **less hype castle, more useful machine.**

## Example Use Cases

### Investigative Work
Build a project pot around a topic, person, case, or event. Save articles, documents, images, notes, transcripts, and links. Let the system help surface entities, overlaps, contradictions, and connection paths.

### Technical Research
Collect documentation, code references, diagrams, papers, issue threads, and implementation notes in one place. Use chat and deep analysis to reduce context switching and keep the whole thing searchable.

### Product and Startup Research
Track competitors, ideas, customer problems, feature references, market signals, and technical options without creating a chaotic graveyard of bookmarks.

### Personal Knowledge System
Use it as a serious second-brain-style environment for things you actually care about, while keeping control over what is stored, what is processed, and what is remembered.
